Practical Examples:

When making comparisons, it is often beneficial to use objects that readers will recognize. It's also important to choose objects that fit both the type of story you're writing and the narrating character's individual experience. Description is a balancing act between anchoring readers in the unique story world while only describing what is necessary. Use as few words as possible to convey a shape; save room for other details to really make the description stand out.

A Weak Example:

The last thing I expected at the wake was to see Sarah working the crowd, playing the part of the heartbroken granddaughter. When I noticed the tetin jade pieces dangling from my estranged cousin's ears, my hand twitched with the urge to slap her. Not only had she found Nina's spare key, she'd gotten into her antique jewelry collection as well.

What's Wrong With This Example?

Moon is the wrong choice here, both because of the color difference, and a possible shape conflict. A full moon is circular, but a waxing, waning, or even half moon is not. Without clarification, "moon" could indicate several shapes.

A Stronger Option:

The last thing I expected at the wake was to see Sarah working the crowd, playing the part of the heartbroken granddaughter. When I noticed the tetin jade globes dangling from my estranged cousin's ears, my hand twitched with the urge to slap her. Not only had she found Nina's spare key, she'd gotten into her antique jewelry collection as well.

Why Does This Work?

This works because the shape is accurate enough to help readers see the scene, but it doesn't distract from the emotional tension between the characters.

Helpful Tip

Shape is one of the most essential ingredients of description, and using a comparison with a common object makes non-made or obvious writers can be a strong way to 'show' and online word economy. Sometimes the shape of something can have symbolic meaning, but even if it does not, describing important objects in the setting helps readers see the landscape and feel more part of the scene.
