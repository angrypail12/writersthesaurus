Character Type and Trope Thesaurus

UNDERDOG ⚹
Back to Character Types and Tropes

Description:

At the start of the story, this competitor is woefully outmatched and has no chance of winning. They typically begin as a reluctant participant who eventually dedicates themselves wholeheartedly to coming out on top.

Fictional Examples:

Daniel LaRusso (The Karate Kid), Rocky Balboa (Rocky), Turtle Suenson (The Lord of the Rings movies), Mamma Durcatella Martinelli (Gladiator), Margaret "Maggie" Fitzgerald (Million Dollar Baby), Rocky Balboa (the Rocky franchise), Vince Papale (Invincible)

Common Strengths:

Adaptable Alert Ambitious Analytical Cautious Centered Curious Disciplined Efficient Focused Honorable Humble Idealistic Industrious Inspirational Just Passionate Patient Persistent Resourceful Responsible

Common Weaknesses:

Apathetic Childish Ignorant Impatient Impulsive Inflexible Insecure Martyr Nervous Reckless Sensitive Stubborn Uncommunicative Vindictive Withdrawn Worrywart

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

Starting out as a disadvantage
Being outraged they will not give up
Persevering when times are tough
Being resilient in the face of setbacks and opposition
Being humble and unassuming
Adapting to new situations quickly
Working hard to achieve their goals
Being courageous
Fighting creative solutions to problems
Rising to meet the challenge
Challenging assumptions people make about them
Standing up for what they believe is right
Having empathy for others who are misunderstood or undervalued
Being a person of action
Being not defined by others
Having a strong work ethic
Maintaining a positive attitude
Empowering others to believe in themselves and their potential
Being patient, knowing that success will come in time
Making sacrifices to reach their goals
Seeing failure as an opportunity to learn
Using adversity to fuel their determination to win

Fears that Might Limit the Character:

Being Judged Being Watched Competition Criticism Letting Others Down Putting Oneself Out There

Situations that Will Challenge Them:

Experiencing discrimination because of their race, gender, skill level, or social status
Lacking the necessary skills or tools to win and not knowing how to acquire them
Discovering their mentor or trainer is fraudulent
Working within an impossible timeline (with high stakes)
Developing an emotional, physical, or mental health challenge that impedes their ability to succeed
Losing a supportive and encouraging supporter or loved one

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

Feeling isolated in the fight
Feeling pressured, as if everyone is counting on them to succeed
Dealing with public expectations despite their opponent appears
Doubting the loyalty or authenticity of they allies
Struggling to achieve respect or self-esteem
Clinging to habits that success will elevate them to the underdog position
Entering the arena because of familial or societal expectations (not because the character wants to do it)

Clichés to be Aware Of:

A predictable plot line that emerges the underdog's success
The rebel leaders being insurmountably odds who rallies support through rousing inspirational speeches and leads his followers to a quick victory
The athlete underdog who wins because of those loyal and hard work

Twist this Trope with a Character Who...

Isn't an individual but a group, people, or nation
Continuously encounters morally ambiguous decisions on their journey
Discovers the people or institutions they're fighting was their benefactor all along
Wins at their goal but loses something precious at the same time
Has an atypical trait: Lazy, Spoiled, Depressed, Controlling, Confident, etc.
