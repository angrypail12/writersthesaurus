SCROOGE

Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. You can also break expectations with an internal job, informative ways, or positive personality change that will help your character stand apart.

Description:

A miserable miser who is too busy seeking money or protecting their wealth to care much about others.

Fictional Examples:

Ebenezer Scrooge (A Christmas Carol), Fagin (Oliver Twist), The Grinch (How the Grinch Stole Christmas), Mr. Potter (It's a Wonderful Life)

Common Strengths:

AMBITIOUS ANALYTICAL DECISIVE DISCIPLINED EFFICIENT FOCUSED INDEPENDENT INDUSTRIOUS METICULOUS ORGANIZED PERSISTENT RESOURCEFUL THRIFTY

Common Weaknesses:

ABRASIVE APATHETIC CALLOUS CONTROLLING CRUEL CYNICAL FINICKY GREEDY GRUMPY HAUGHTY INFLEXIBLE JUDGMENTAL OBSESSIVE POSSESSIVE PRETENTIOUS SELFISH STINGY HUMORLESS TACTLESS UNCOOPERATIVE WORKAHOLIC

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Being shrewd and clever

• Having a knack for making money

• Being miserly and methodical

• Obsessing about acquiring wealth over all else

• Being miserly and impersonal

• Despising personal they consider frivolous or unnecessary

• Hoarding possessions and money

• Working constantly

• Lacking compassion or empathy for others

• Being essential to their work

• Being material and social difficulty (they're irresponsible, they're to blame for their situation, etc.)

• Despising the joy of others, especially when it's found in simple things or relationships

• Exploiting people when the opportunity arises

• Being unsympathetic

• Living alone (or with another Scrooge)

• Using critical, harsh, and cruel language

• Demanding the sentimental value of things

• Remaining others have inferior motives

• Being unwilling to spend money on the basics

• Locking and distributing holiday spirit or Christmas-stress

• Not taking pains with their appearance (because fixing so costs money)

• Being unpredictable

• Only putting with their money when a greater return is likely

• Having nothing to do with anything consumptory

• Being stingy or with their appearance if the character doesn't intervene

• Holding grudges

• Controlling others through money

• Being suspicious or with another Scrooge

• Having others based on their financial worth or their ability to add to the character's bank account

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

BECOMING WHAT ONE HATES BEING JUDGED CRITICISM HAVING NO PURPOSE LETTING OTHERS DOWN LOSING FINANCIAL SECURITY NEVER FINDING HAPPINESS REJECTION

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

• Being shown kindness by someone they've been cruel to

• Losing their wealth or status and having to rely on others for help

• Being confronted with how their own selfishness is really caused harmful

• Being reminded of how they younger selves would feel about their current character

• Being told they care about because of their miserly ways

• Recognizing a tragedy that their money can't fix

• Encountering a beggar or poor person who reminds them of themselves

• Attempting to make amends for the consequences of their greed and being rebuffed

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

• Feeling like they should be more generous but not really wanting to change

• Wanting to help someone to need but having the loss of control that comes with becoming their grip on their finances

• Desperately needing something money can't buy (such as companionship, the importance of health, their desire to remain included, etc.)

Cliches to Be Aware Of:

• The old, cantry, humorless who is transformed after a single night of supernatural intervention

• The wildly wealthy miser living in squalor because they refuse to spend a cent on anything unnecessary

• A Scrooge who encounters someone to save who the character doesn't intervene

• The miser who leaves an employee, nephew, or sibling heartily, but that person remains loyal to the character, believing that there is good in them

Twist This Trope With A Character Who...

• ... hoards but not money

• ... navigates the Scrooge arc in reverse—from compassionate and generous to cruel and miserly

• ... is an unreliable narrator, making the reader question whether their transformation is sincere

• ... inherited their wealth and hasn't worked to earn it

• ... experienced a body swap or rash revival with someone they're mistreated as a means of conferming them with their evil ways

• ... has an atypical trait: Charming, Frivolous, Billy, Violent, Courteous, etc.
