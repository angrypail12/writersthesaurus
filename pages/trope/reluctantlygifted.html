RELUCTANTLY GIFTED ✪

Description:

This character is extraordinary in some way, with a supernatural gift or exceptional talent they're reluctant to use.

Fictional Examples:

Rogue (Rogue One: A Star Wars Story), Elsa (Frozen), Nina Zenik (Six of Crows), Adam Parrish (The Raven Cycle), Elsa (Frozen)

Common Strengths:

Cautious Courageous Disciplined Empathetic Focused Honorable Humble Idealistic Independent Industrious Intelligent Mature Protective Responsible Sensible Talented Wise

Common Weaknesses:

Addictive Cowardly Evasive Fussy Hypocritical Insecure Manipulative Megalomaniac Nervous Pessimistic Self-Destructive Selfish Stubborn Suspicious Uncooperative Weak-Willed

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Being a social hiding-place
  • Following their intuition
  • Being secretive
  • Avoiding the spotlight
  • Trying to blend in
  • Being ordinary
  • Underplaying their abilities and skills
  • Having a strong moral code
  • Being reluctant to use their full power
  • Turning toward reluctance
  • Wanting to remain in their comfort zone
  • Finding reasons not to use their gift
  • Using their gift reluctantly
  • Resenting their choice and seeing them as a burden
  • Feeling personally responsible for things
  • Being a loner
  • Trying to move
  • Having a life they'd prefer to being extraordinary
  • Not wanting to be responsible for others
  • Hiding their gift
  • Being cynical
  • Thinking in terms of worst-case scenarios
  • Assuming that bad things that happened before will happen again

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

A Loved One Dying Becoming What One Hates Being Capable of Harm Being Judged Being Labeled Being Watched Being Separated from Loved Ones Being Unable to Achieve a Dream Being Taken Advantage Of Being Viewed Government Isolation Letting Others Down Losing Autonomy Losing the Respect of Others Persecution Relational Commitment Trusting Others

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

  • A loved one putting their life at risk and the character's gift being the cure they can do to save or try using their gift
  • Having their gift exposed
  • Being tricked into using their talents for nefarious purposes
  • Being forced to use their power
  • Being publicly rejected for hiding their abilities for talent
  • New legislation that makes using their gift or ability illegal or punishable by death
  • Not being able to control their power
  • Losing their ability

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

  • Remembering when they've used their gift in the past with bad results
  • Knowing that using their gift should be for the past with bad news
  • Wrestling if people find out what they can do, so they'll be ostracized
  • Worrying that if people find out what they can do, they'll be ostracized
  • Feeling their gift is corrupted
  • Feeling guilty for not having the courage to use their talent or being unable to control it
  • Worrying that they can't live up to other people's expectations
  • Knowing that using their gift will cost them something personally
  • Knowing that people find out where they can do things they'll be stressed
  • Knowing that using their gift will aid them something personally
  • Aware that if people find out where they can do things, they'll be stressed

Clichés to Be Aware Of:

  • The character whose powers come without limits
  • The chosen one who may have powers they must use to save the world
  • The reluctantly gifted loner
  • The role model who comes along and who helps heal in the story

Twist This Trope With a Character Who...

  • Has always been a gift and a curse
  • Has problems their ability can't solve
  • Uses their gift when it serves their own purposes
  • Has more than one hidden gift
  • Uses their gift to solve fun or comedic situations
  • Has an atypical trait: Drunkemontic, Cooky, Vindicative, etc.

Create Something New:

  • Reluctantly Gifted + Bad Boy
  • Reluctantly Gifted + Femme Fatale
  • Reluctantly Gifted + Bully
  • Reluctantly Gifted + Savant
  • Reluctantly Gifted + Pioneer

Similar or Related Tropes:

Rebel (Archetype) Reluctant Hero Reluctant Monster Tortured Hero

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
