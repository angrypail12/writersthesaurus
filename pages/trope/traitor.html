TRAITOR ⚹
Back to Character Types and Tropes

Description:

At some point in the story, this character perceives issues and betrays their former allies to benefit themselves.

Fictional Examples:

Cyrus Lupo(arrel (A Song of Ice and Fire), Edmund Pevensie (The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe), Peter Pettigrew (the Harry Potter stories), Iago (Othello), Judas (the Bible)

Common Strengths:

Adaptable Adventurous Alert Ambitious Analytical Cautious Charming Diplomatic Discreet Observant Patient Persuasive Resourceful Responsible

Common Weaknesses:

Cowardly Defensive Devious Dishonest Disloyal Hypocritical Manipulative Nosy Selfish Suspicious

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

Being adept at reading others and knowing what they want to hear
Thinking strategically
Being slow to offer trustworthiness to others
Resenting or keeping a low profile
Being highly observant, watching everyone and seeing everything
Being suspicious of others
Easily distrusting other people, unwilling to doubt they have about the character
Living a double life
Acting noncommittal or ambiguous questions to figure out where people stand
Lying easily
Delivering well-timed support or betrayal at a destination
Manipulating others for their own gain
Taking either short-sighted or extraordinarily long-sighted
Taking actions without care for how they will hurt others
Acting in morally ambiguous ways
Being envious
Raising distrust while maintaining a presence of loyalty
Playing their cards close to the vest
Being devious
Looking out for their own interests first and foremost

Fears that Might Limit the Character:

A Secret Being Revealed Becoming What One Hates Being Judged Being Taken Advantage Of Betrayal Isolation Losing The Respect Of Others One's Instincts Being Unreliable Persecution Rejection

Situations that Will Challenge Them:

Being betrayed by someone they've allied with
Being caught in their betrayal
Being assigned a professional duty that conflicts with their personal motives
Their intentions being prematurely revealed to the person they intended to betray
Discovering unintended and possibly catastrophic consequences of their betrayal
Having to work with those they intended to betray to neutralize an external threat
Discovering new information that changes their perspective and makes them rethink their loyalties

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

Being torn between self-preservation and doing what's right
Feeling guilty for their betrayals and rationalizing why it was their best option
Wanting to make amends but facing external pressure to abandon all contact with the betrayed party
Yearning to be accepted by the people they're supposed to betray
Living in constant fear that their betrayal and true motivations will be discovered
Struggling with indecision about an upcoming betrayal

Clichés to be Aware Of:

The traitor being completely callous toward those they betray
The one-dimensional traitor who is portrayed as purely evil-serving with no conscience
The betrayer who suddenly switches sides at the last moment, often by heroically sacrificing themselves, with little buildup or justification so it feels forced
The traitor whose motivations are predictable, such as revenge or envy

Twist this Trope with a Character Who...

Never intended to betray their allies but their plans went awry or their actions were misinterpreted
Is only pretending to be a traitor to gain the trust of a powerful enemy
Regrets their decision
Betrays their second group of allies in favour of returning to the original group they betrayed
Is being coerced into betraying their allies to protect someone they care about
Is not in their right mind due to being hypnotized, possessed, or brainwashed
Has an atypical trait: Honorable, Sentimental, Remorseful, Worried, Wholesome, etc.
