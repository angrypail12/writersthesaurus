RELUCTANT HERO ✪

Description:

This protagonist doesn't want to be a hero and actively works to avoid becoming one. Throughout the course of their arc, they eventually embrace and grow into their role as the hero of the story.

Fictional Examples:

Frodo Baggins (The Lord of the Rings franchise), Katniss Everdeen (The Hunger Games trilogy), Violet Mercier (The Nightingale), Sarah Connor (The Terminator)

Common Strengths:

Adaptable Alert Cautious Courageous Empathetic Generous Honorable Humble Independent Introverted Just Loyal Pensive Resourceful Responsible Sensible Talented

Common Weaknesses:

Apathetic Cowardly Cynical Evasive Hostile Indecisive Nervous Paranoid Selfish Stubborn Suspicious Timid Uncooperative Worrywart

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Being self-reliant
  • Having high moral standards
  • Deflecting being the center of attention
  • Preferring to remain anonymous
  • Preferring to be seen as ordinary
  • Being self-aware or self-centered (initially)
  • Preferring quiet life at home
  • Being self-spoken and mild-mannered
  • Turning on emotions
  • Keeping any unusual skills or interests a secret
  • Dreading change in routine or habits
  • Overthinking things
  • Fearing the unknown
  • Sometimes over-thinking their actions
  • Difficulty accepting compliments
  • Not having the greatest social skills (being shy, looking lost, etc.)
  • Keeping appropriate responses to themselves
  • Clinging to their comfort zone
  • Being socially awkward
  • Tending toward skepticism
  • Having commitment issues
  • Under-achieving

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

Being Responsible for Others Being Unable to Achieve a Dream Being Watched Criticism Failure Leading Letting Others Down Losing Autonomy Public Speaking Putting Oneself Out There Rejection

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

  • Having to decide to engage in a difficult situation or do nothing
  • A loved one being mortal peril
  • Witnessing something horrifying a burden for the character not to act
  • Others acknowledging the character's weakness and pushing them to change
  • A leader vacating their role, creating an opportunity for the character to step up
  • Being held responsible for a past failure
  • Being accused of cowardice or hypocrisy
  • Their comfort zone being destroyed, removing it as an option for the character

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

  • Struggling with imposter syndrome
  • Knowing that their actions will cause collateral damage
  • Remembering a past moment when they should have acted but didn't
  • Fears that they aren't the person they once tried to be
  • Knowing they should take on the leadership role but being too afraid to leave their comfort zone
  • Facing an ethical dilemma where there's no good option
  • Being paralyzed by the weight of other people's expectations
  • Feeling crushed by the weight of other people's expectations
  • Being paralyzed by self-doubt

Clichés to Be Aware Of:

  • The cynical and pessimistic reluctant hero
  • The reluctant hero who becomes too complacent too quickly
  • The character making an abrupt personal from reluctant to fully committed hero
  • The reluctant hero who is given powers or other key character is killed

Twist This Trope With a Character Who...

  • Has a good reason for their reluctance but does learned improperly or stubbornly
  • Has skeletons in their closet
  • Believes after accepting their role that they're being used as a pawn to further someone else's agenda
  • Embraces the hero role only partially, or their own terms, rather than making an about-face and jumping into it wholeheartedly
  • Has an atypical trait: Flamboyant, Psychic, Vindictive, Whiny, Witty, etc.

Create Something New:

  • Reluctant Hero + Tragic Hero
  • Reluctant Hero + Sage
  • Reluctant Hero + Hot Billionaire
  • Reluctant Hero + Innocent
  • Reluctant Hero + Villain

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
