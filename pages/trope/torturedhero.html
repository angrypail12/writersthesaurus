TORTURED HERO

Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. You can also break expectations with an internal job, informative ways, or positive personality change that will help your character stand apart.

Description:

This deeply emotional hero is characterized by self-doubt, melancholy, and isolation. In extreme cases, they may be depressed or self-destructive.

Fictional Examples:

Allies (The Time Traveler's Wife), Sebastian (Crime and Punishment), Esther Greenwood (The Bell Jar), Katniss Everdeen (The Hunger Games), the Beast (Beauty and the Beast)

Common Strengths:

ANALYTICAL CAUTIOUS EMPATHETIC INTELLIGENT INTROVERTED LOYAL OBSERVANT PASSIONATE PENSIVE PERCEPTIVE PHILOSOPHICAL PRIVATE SENTIMENTAL STUDIOUS WISE

Common Weaknesses:

ANTISOCIAL CYNICAL DEFENSIVE DISHONEST EVASIVE IRRATIONAL MARTYR MELODRAMATIC NEEDY OBSESSIVE OVERSENSITIVE PESSIMISTIC RECKLESS RESENTFUL SELF-DESTRUCTIVE SELF-INDULGENT SELFISH

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Hiding relying justice

• Thinking carefully before they speak

• Doubting themselves

• Daydreaming of a simpler life

• Being able to get themselves in other people about

• Seeking the truth

• Being emotionally reserved

• Struggling with painful memories

• Ignoring past traumas and facing their demons

• Shock understanding (or just being reluctant to be around)

• Having a past problem

• Having unreconciled to others

• Spending a lot of time in their own head

• Slipping easily into depression and gloom

• Struggling with addictions

• Struggling when being someone required

• Being apathetic

• Difficulty committing to others

• Always being prepared

• Always worrying about what could go wrong

• Thinking negative thoughts

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

A SECRET BEING REVEALED BECOMING WHAT ONE HATES BEING CAPABLE OF HARM BEING PHYSICALLY TOUCHED BEING SEPARATED FROM LOVED ONES BETRAYAL CRITICISM DEATH FAILURE HAVING NO PURPOSE HOPE LETTING OTHERS DOWN REJECTION RELATIONAL COMMITMENT TRUSTING OTHERS

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

• Facing a difficult moral choice in which there is no clear answer

• Facing a situation that echoes their wounding event

• Being challenged by someone who cares about their well-being

• Being forced into a position of leadership

• Suffering yet another love that threatens to push them over the edge emotionally

• Being forced into a relationship with someone they care about

• Having to forge opinions and hope to keep someone's spirits up

• Meeting someone who challenges their entire ego, encouraging them to leave their comfort zone and face their past

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

• Being to keep it together for the people around them while struggling inside

• Suffering from impostor syndrome

• Struggling with deep feelings of guilt

• Wating to use others as their salvation

• Doubting redemption but believing it beyond their reach

• Wanting company or companionship but feeling they don't deserve it

• Being convinced that life is meaningless but hoping they're wrong

• Struggling with an increased sense critic

Cliches to Be Aware Of:

• The whiny hero who loves feeling sorry for himself

• The tortured hero who won't help themselves or accept help from others

• The hero who is saved by love

• The apathetic hero who is too quick gained into action

Twist This Trope With A Character Who...

• ... isn't tortured by a trauma

• ... isn't male

• ... is surrounded and social

• ... takes their melancholy apparent on world mood rather than a wounding event

• ... has an atypical trait: Charming, Quirky, Randy, Witty, etc.

CHILDREN'S ENTERTAINER LIBRARIAN NANNY PERSONAL ASSISTANT TO A CELEBRITY WEDDING PLANNER YOGA INSTRUCTOR
