STARVING ARTIST

Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. You can also break expectations with an internal job, informative ways, or positive personality change that will help your character stand apart.

Description:

This character artist is dedicated to a craft that doesn't meet their financial needs. They are often plagued by self-doubt and struggle to balance artistic endeavors with real-world responsibilities.

Fictional Examples:

Orpheus (Greek mythology), Stephen Dedalus (A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man), Cliff Stern (Crimes and Misdemeanors)

Common Strengths:

AMBITIOUS CREATIVE DISCIPLINED FOCUSED IDEALISTIC IMAGINATIVE INDEPENDENT INTROVERTED OBSERVANT PASSIONATE PENSIVE PERSISTENT PHILOSOPHICAL QUIRKY RESOURCEFUL TALENTED THRIFTY

Common Weaknesses:

ADDICTIVE ANTISOCIAL COMPULSIVE DISHONEST EVASIVE IRRESPONSIBLE JEALOUS NEEDY OBSESSIVE PESSIMISTIC RESENTFUL SELF-DESTRUCTIVE STUBBORN TEMPERAMENTAL WITHDRAWN WORKAHOLIC

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Spending the evenings and populate mornings

• Searching for inspiration in their sense

• Being frugal and thrifty

• Being intensely dedicated to their artistic practice

• Putting an emphasis on artistic vision over financial success or sleep

• Romanticizing; being able to make do or fix things themselves

• Taking risks with their work

• Being more focused on the people around them

• Doing odd jobs and side gigs to pay the bills

• Always being on the lookout for inspiration

• Spending long hours on their work

• Networking and encouragement and being constantly hosed by self-doubt

• Being disciplined about their work

• Complaining about their inability to catch a break

• Blaming others for their lack of success

• Missing deadlines for their level of success

• Feeling misunderstood

• Wanting toward non-conformity

• Having strong ideals about how art should be done

• Embracing unusual beheld or ideas; being seen by others as odd or "not there"

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

A SECRET BEING REVEALED BEING TAKEN TO ACHIEVE A DREAM BEING UNABLE TO ACHIEVE A DREAM CERTAIN KINDS OF PEOPLE COMPETITION CRITICISM FAILURE BEYOND OTHERS DOWN LOSING THE RESPECT OF OTHERS MEDIOCRITY

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

• A client friend or loved one becoming more successful than the character

• Having to take a hated day job

• A publisher, editor, or agent pushing the character to compromise their standards for money

• Being betrayed or ripped off by someone they trust or their dreams

• Experiencing discrimination that keeps the artist from being accepted

• Struggling with an addiction

• Being forced to sell their art for below-market value

• A spouse of loved one being tired in the classroom

• Losing a mentor

• Going through a dry open where inspiration deserts the character

• A marketplace being rejected

• Accidentally destroying a massive amount of their work

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

• Being jealous of successful artists

• The character wanting to do what's right for them but also needing the approval of a parent, sibling, or significant other

• Being given a shot at someone that comes at someone else's expense

• Being forced to choose between pursuing their passion and being a job that brings much needed income

• Struggling with imposter syndrome

• Struggling with important personal relationships

• Wrestling with an untreated mental health condition

Cliches to Be Aware Of:

• The artist who romanticizes poverty and doesn't seem to be affected much by it

• The artist experiencing a breakthrough and effortlessly creating a masterpiece

• The stereotypical artist living in an expensive area, such as Paris, New York, or Los Angeles

• A depressed, drug-addicted, and/or suicidal artist

• A struggling artist living in a loft studio-type residence

• The artist who makes a living as a furniture and unethical businessperson

Twist This Trope With A Character Who...

• ... had one early success but hasn't been able to replicate it

• ... is extremely successful in their day job, which is unrelated to their art

• ... is compelled to create art but is disturbing or infuriating to others

• ... looks and lives like the typical suburbanite

• ... has an atypical trait: Cary, Devoted, Manipulative, Superstitious, Traditional, Sensible, etc.

Unexpected Occupations for This Character:

AUTO MECHANIC BOUNCER BOUNTY HUNTER CLERGY MEMBER
