SAVANT ✪

Description:

Savants are gifted in a certain field but deficient in other areas. They might have studied hard to hone their talent, or the skill may come naturally with little work or practice at all.

Fictional Examples:

Sherlock Holmes (the Sherlock Holmes series), Beth Harmon (The Queen's Gambit), Raymond Babbitt (Rain Man), Sheridan Cooper (The Big Bang Theory)

Common Strengths:

Creative Curious Decisive Focused Imaginative Independent Introverted Meticulous Passionate Pensive Persistent Private Quirky Studious Talented

Common Weaknesses:

Abrasive Apathetic Compulsive Disorganized Forgetful Fussy Humorless Impatient Inflexible Obsessive Tactless Temperamental Uncommunicative Uncooperative Withdrawn

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Being exceptionally talented or gifted in a particular area of knowledge
  • Focusing intently on one thing for long periods of time
  • Striving for mastery in their area of expertise
  • Being a perfectionist
  • Preferring to work alone
  • Identifying patterns easily or subconsciously
  • Being extremely organized or being disorganized, or moving fluidly with everyday tasks beyond their scope of genius
  • Not picking up on social cues
  • Lacking emotional empathy
  • Having memorable quirks
  • Being socially awkward
  • Difficulty relating to others
  • Having a difficult time forming social connections
  • Displaying compulsive behaviors
  • Having limited interests
  • Being uncomfortable with their own emotions
  • Being socially awkward
  • Maintaining a rigid routine
  • Having heightened sensory sensitivity
  • Struggling with change

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

Becoming What One Hates Being Labeled Being Unable to Achieve a Dream Competition Discrimination Losing One's Mind Rejection

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
