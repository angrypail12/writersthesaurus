RIGHTFUL RULER ✪

Description:

In a kingdom that going from bad to worse, this character has a legitimate claim to the throne and is the only one who can make things right. But first they'll have to come out of protective hiding, stop running from fate, or be made aware of their destiny. The early part of the character's arc is the focus of this entry. For information on this character once they've become the ruler they were meant to be, see GOOD KING OR QUEEN.

Fictional Examples:

Aragorn (The Lord of the Rings), King Arthur (The Once and Future King), Daenerys Targaryen (A Song of Ice and Fire), King T'Challa (the Marvel Universe), Queen Elsa (Frozen)

Common Strengths:

Adventurous Calm Cautious Charming Discreet Easygoing Empathetic Honorable Humble Inspirational Intelligent Just Kind Loyal Observant Patient Perceptive Persuasive Private Protective Responsible Socially Aware Tolerant Traditional Wise Witty

Common Weaknesses:

Apathetic Childish Cowardly Defensive Dishonest Evasive Haughty Hypocritical Impatient Inflexible Insecure Indecisive Perfectionist Pessimistic Temperamental Weak-Willed Withdrawn Worrywart

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Valuing justice
  • Putting the needs of many over their own
  • Being courteous and respectful
  • Seeking to right wrongs
  • Making sacrifices in the name of justice or doing the right thing
  • Being naturally liked and respected by others
  • Being groomed with honor and holding themselves to a high standard
  • Listening to wise counsel
  • Having a strong personal code of honor
  • Showing compassion and mercy to others
  • Being humble, avoiding activities or actions that will draw unneeded attention
  • Seeking out more deserving leaders before accepting leadership responsibilities
  • Connecting with all land or their people (emotionally or supernaturally)
  • Showing glimpses of wisdom
  • Thinking of the community as a team, not as individuals
  • Being eloquent, charming, charismatic, or inspiring
  • Standing up for their people in small, understated ways
  • Encouraging others
  • Becoming defensive when pushed toward their destiny
  • Avoiding people who know the character's true identity or destiny
  • Making excuses for why they won't claim the throne
  • Seeing things the way they want to see them instead of how they actually are (no they can justify not stepping up and taking charge)

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

A Secret Being Revealed Becoming What One Hates Being Responsible for Others Betrayal Criticism Failure Leading Letting Others Down Losing Autonomy Not Being in Control

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

  • Someone threatening to reveal the character's whereabouts or the truth of their identity
  • Encountering their supposed supporters
  • Losing the support of an ally
  • Being pressured to go public before the character is willing to do so
  • Trying to effect change without an official authority and being ridiculed for it
  • Having to sacrifice rules or learn necessary skills while being scrutinized by others
  • Being challenged as an outsider
  • Learning that they can only regain their positions at the sacrifice of their integrity

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

  • Doubting their ability or right to rule
  • Having dreams and aspirations that keeps the character away from their destiny
  • Self-doubt related to their past and what caused the fall from power
  • Knowing what they must do but fearing failure
  • Wanting to share their secret with someone but not knowing who can be trusted

Clichés to Be Aware Of:

  • The unschooled king whose followers support him with unwavering loyalty based solely on his bloodline
  • The ruler who, once claiming their birthright, too easily learns the lessons and skills needed to be an effective leader
  • The righteous ruler whose virtue and honor is treated only by the blindness of their opponents heart

Twist This Trope With a Character Who...

  • Has admirable motivations for wanting to take the throne but is deeply flawed
  • Is not the lost heir or hasn't been magically chosen
  • Is not related to those in power, and on-lands because it's stronger than one
  • Is fulfilled and wisely successful in their current life, making it harder for them to give it up
  • Truly doesn't believe they're the rightful ruler
  • Has an atypical trait: Machel, Tomei, Flamboyant, Foolish, Devious, etc.

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
