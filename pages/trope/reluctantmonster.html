RELUCTANT MONSTER ✪

Description:

A reluctant monster is assumed to be evil and scary by nature, but that isn't who they are. In some cases, they don't know they're a monster at all.

Fictional Examples:

The BFG (The BFG), Frankenstein's Monster (Frankenstein), Quasimodo (The Hunchback of Notre Dame), Edward Scissorhands (Edward Scissorhands), Shrek (Shrek), King Kong (King Kong)

Common Strengths:

Affectionate Cooperative Empathetic Friendly Generous Gentle Honest Honorable Humble Innocent Kind Observant Perceptive Protective Quirky Resourceful Simple Trusting Unselfish

Common Weaknesses:

Antisocial Cowardly Devious Foolish Grumpy Hostile Inflexible Insecure Needy Rebellious Reckless Self-Destructive Timid Unintelligent Volatile Weak-Willed Withdrawn

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Being open-minded
  • Not conforming to other people's expectations
  • Being empathic
  • Feeling empathy for others
  • Not wanting to stand out in a crowd
  • Being lonely
  • Trying hard not to hurt others
  • Not trusting their own strength
  • Being careful
  • Exerting self-control to quell their monster-like impulses or abilities
  • Craving acceptance
  • Being naive
  • Feeling self-conscious about their appearance
  • Finding joy in simple things
  • Wanting to help others
  • Responding to people and situations in unusual ways
  • Not being worldly or street smart
  • Not accepting things the way they are
  • Avoiding crowds and social situations
  • Being ashamed of himself and people who are able to live a "normal" life
  • Being judged by humans and people who are able to live "normally"
  • Hashtening to speak out or stand up for themselves
  • Lacking confidence
  • Being responsible for people looking to take advantage of someone

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

Becoming What One Hates Being Attacked Being Capable of Harm Being Labeled Being Taken Advantage Of Being Unable to Achieve a Dream Being Unsafe Change Humiliation Isolation Leaving No Legacy Letting Others Down Losing Autonomy Never Finding Happiness Not Fitting In Persecution

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

  • Being cast out of their group
  • Being persecuted or feared in the human world
  • Being pushed into conflict when they've been hiding what they are or if
  • Living under a curse but being unable to break it
  • Accidentally hurting someone
  • Being mistle to control their monster-like impulses
  • Needing the use their monster qualities to help someone but knowing that will alienate them even more
  • Being judged because of their name, family history, or other perceived wrongdoing

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

  • Feeling isolated from not being accepted in either the monster or human worlds
  • Struggling with their compulsion to attack others, destroy things, or creat mayhem
  • Feeling like they don't belong in their own group but knowing they won't be accepted elsewhere
  • Having a destructive or consuming drive, even though they desperately want to be good
  • Feeling like a threat
  • Wanting they could help someone about what they're going through
  • Feeling like they're in the wrong body
  • Struggling with vengeful and vicious feelings brought on by being bullied and misunderstood

Clichés to Be Aware Of:

  • The reluctant monster that doesn't believe they're a monster or struggles with the season of who they are
  • The monster that is more compassionate to accept people than by a bully or which point they go berserk
  • A monster who is saved by love
  • The love interest who doesn't fear the monster or being hurt by them

Twist This Trope With a Character Who...

  • Has agency in the story
  • Has control in the story
  • Is a leader in their own group or species
  • Has a regular appearance but is mastering on the inside
  • Has a quirky or unusual way of connecting with humans
  • Has an atypical trait: Flamboyant, Naughty, Pompous, Gumming, Wise, etc.

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
