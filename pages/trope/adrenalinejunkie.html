ADRENALINE JUNKIE
Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character. But mapping them outside of typical expectations helps characters stand out and become memorable, not stereotypical. When used with purpose, every trope can become a compelling character.

Description

The adrenaline junkie is driven by a need for stimulation and excitement. The more intense or dangerous the situation is, the more likely they are to participate.

Fictional Examples

Johnny Utah (Point Break), Tony (The Lord of the Rings), Starbuck (Battlestar Galactica), Tyler Durden (Fight Club), and Marla Singer (Fight Club).

Common Strengths

ADVENTUROUS BOLD CONFIDENT COURAGEOUS CREATIVE CURIOUS DECISIVE FOCUSED IMAGINATIVE INDEPENDENT PASSIONATE PERSISTENT PROACTIVE SPONTANEOUS

Common Weaknesses

ANTISOCIAL COMPULSIVE DISORGANIZED IMPULSIVE IRRESPONSIBLE RECKLESS SELF-DESTRUCTIVE STUBBORN

Situations That Will Challenge Them

Tedium in their routine work
Situations that require consistently doing the same activity as a bureaucracy
Caring for others and having others care for them (one to three year job)
Functioning and working for others
Settling in relationships to avoid/settle their own desires
Accepting a failure or injury that restricts their mobility (a giant metal wall that's too high, navigating a wheelchaired kid without a girlfriend, etc.)
