SAGE (ARCHETYPE) ✪

Description:

Sages are wise, always seeking knowledge, but they also are willing to impart their learnings to others. This makes them ideal mentors and parental figures (and subtly different from MAGICIANS).

Fictional Examples:

Mother Abigail (The Stand), Gus Grissom (The Once and Future King), Obi-Wan Kenobi (the Star Wars franchise), Mr. Miyagi (The Karate Kid), Mr. Keating (Dead Poet's Society)

Common Strengths:

Calm Centered Confident Cooperative Curious Decisive Disciplined Discreet Generous Gentle Honorable Intelligent Just Kind Loyal Mature Observant Philosophical Protective Resourceful Talented Uninhibited Unselfish Wise

Common Weaknesses:

Controlling Fussy Know-It-All Perfectionist Possessive Pushy Worrisome Worrywart

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Seeking truth
  • Being a lifelong learner
  • Having an eye for talent that others lack
  • Being specialized in an area of expertise (the Force, magic, math, baseball, etc.)
  • Being quirky
  • Holding a job that is well-below their potential because it allows them to help others
  • Considering different viewpoints instead of dismissing or being threatened by them
  • Probing into new ideas
  • Thinking before speaking; weighing their thoughts and words carefully
  • Being a good teacher
  • Having a talent for making learning accessible
  • Using unorthodox teaching methods
  • Being optimistic about people and the future of humanity
  • Being perceptive about people
  • Challenging lies, misinformation, and hatred
  • Being non-judgmental
  • Thinking deeply about things
  • Working well with others
  • Not being ego-driven (learning isn't about getting credit for ideas, etc.)
  • Taking a hands-off approach; not actively participating in the fight
  • Being reluctant to take action until enough knowledge is gained
  • Making personal sacrifices in the pursuit of knowledge
  • Sharing knowledge for the greater good
  • Having a spiritual or mystical bent
  • Guiding other characters to find their own answers (instead of telling them often to believe)
  • Being perceptive—particularly regarding the hero's weaknesses and how they should be challenged
  • Responding to threats calmly and carefully
  • Thinking in the long term
  • Being calm and thoughtful when choosing course of action

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

Being Responsible for Others Having No Purpose Letting Others Down Losing One's Mind One's Instincts Being Unreliable Relational Commitment Trusting Others

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
