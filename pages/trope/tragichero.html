TRAGIC HERO

Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. You can also break expectations with an internal job, informative ways, or positive personality change that will help your character stand apart.

Description:

This character shares many of the traditionally heroic traits: courage, honor, nobility, ambition, and perseverance. But despite their good intentions, they have a fatal flaw that they're unable to overcome and results in their ultimate demise.

Fictional Examples:

Hamlet (Hamlet), Jay Gatsby (The Great Gatsby), Carmen Santangelo (Game of Thrones), Tony Soprano (The Sopranos), Anakin Skywalker (the Star Wars franchise)

Common Strengths:

ADAPTABLE AMBITIOUS BOLD CONFIDENT DECISIVE FOCUSED HONORABLE IDEALISTIC INDEPENDENT INTELLIGENT LOYAL OBSERVANT PASSIONATE PENSIVE PERCEPTIVE PERSISTENT PROTECTIVE RESOURCEFUL

Common Weaknesses:

ABRASIVE CALLOUS CHILDISH COCKY COMPULSIVE CONFRONTATIONAL CONTROLLING DEVIOUS FANATICAL GREEDY HYPOCRITICAL IMPULSIVE IRRATIONAL MANIPULATIVE OBSESSIVE POSSESSIVE SELF-DESTRUCTIVE STUBBORN VOLATILE

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Taking any risk if it will bring the closer to their goal

• Being responsible

• Trending toward introspection (and possibly over-thinking)

• Having a strong code of beliefs

• Working well with partners, underlings, and others

• Preferring to work alone

• Thinking carefully before they speak

• Believing they have a purpose, and working toward it

• Enjoying a challenge

• Being driven to do things for the good of their loved ones

• Having a healthy work ethic; doing what needs to be done to accomplish the goal

• Being age-driven

• Struggling with personal demons

• Arrogance

• Hiding or downplaying their true feelings

• Lacking self-awareness

• Working hard to keep their flaws or demons hidden

• Rationalizing

• Becoming obsessed or fanatical about their goal

• Being too proud to ask their own team and shortcomings

• Believing themselves to be bullet-proof

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

ABANDONMENT BECOMING WHAT ONE HATES BEING A BURDEN TO OTHERS BEING CAPABLE OF HARM BETRAYAL CERTAIN KINDS OF PEOPLE DEATH HUMILIATION LEAVING NO LEGACY LETTING OTHERS DOWN LOSING THE RESPECT OF OTHERS NOT BEING IN CONTROL NOT FITTING IN REJECTION RELATIONAL COMMITMENT TRUSTING OTHERS

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

• Having to make a difficult moral or ethical choice that will hurt them or someone else

• Being confronted with their flaw

• Failing with addiction

• Being betrayed by someone close to them

• Having their knowledge base discovered and threatened to be revealed

• Having to work with someone they don't respect or trust

• Being thrust into a position of power they don't want or feel ready for

• Facing a rival who is stronger or smarter than they are

• Being reminded of their first fear

• Being confronted with the people about their care about

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

• Fearing that they can't protect the people who are close to them

• Being aware of their flaws but being unable to resist or change them

• Being haunted by sad and ashamed of their past

• Being aware that their actions are hurting others but continuing to pursue a cause

• Being plagued by deep insecurity and being driven to hide it from others

• Resenting their fatal flaw but choosing to ignore or dismiss it

• Recognizing that a past wounding event is destroying but refusing to come to terms with it

Cliches to Be Aware Of:

• The tragic hero whose downfall is too predictable

• The tragic hero whose world is too simplistic (black vs. white, good vs. evil, etc.)

• The tragic hero whose death or downfall transformation occurs too quickly or easily

Twist This Trope With A Character Who...

• ... is a child or a teenager

• ... isn't male

• ... isn't afraid to show their vulnerabilities

• ... has a sense of dark that plays a key role in their downfall

• ... has an atypical trait: Affectionate, Frivolous, Funny, Mischievous, Spontaneous, etc.
