Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. Description:

Snobs look down on people below their own social or financial station. They often display an exaggerated sense of elitism and condescension because they believe they're superior in some way.

Fictional Examples:

The Malfoys (Harry Potter series), Lady Catherine de Bourgh (Pride and Prejudice), Tom Buchanan (The Great Gatsby), Regina George (Mean Girls), Frasier and Niles Crane (Frasier)

Common Strengths:

AMBITIOUS CENTERED CHARMING CONFIDENT METICULOUS PASSIONATE PROPER SOPHISTICATED

Common Weaknesses:

CATTY CRUEL FINICKY GOSSIPY HAUGHTY HUMORLESS INFLEXIBLE JUDGMENTAL KNOW-IT-ALL MATERIALISTIC NOSY PREJUDICED PRETENTIOUS SELF-INDULGENT SPOILED VAIN

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Having a strong sense of personal identity

• Not being easily swayed by the opinions of others

• Paying meticulous attention to detail

• Being concerned with image and presentation

• Being self-possessed and appearing confident

• Being driven to achieve, greater success and importance through self-development

• Having discerning tastes

• Having a deep understanding about their area of interests—art, fashion, literature, or even mundane things, like coffee or wine

• Being adept at social manipulation to get what they want

• Indulging in extravagant displays of wealth or personal achievement

• Putting others down—publicly or privately—because of a lack of personal taste

• Having a superiority complex

• Belittling other friends or wealth makes them wealthier or more supported than others

• Expecting that they can improve their social status

• Dropping names

• Attending themselves to people who can improve their status

• Looking for clues into people's opinions and criticisms

• Expounding or their opinions whenever possible

• Excelling at finding weak spots and attacking them
