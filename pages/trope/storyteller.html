STORYTELLER

Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. You can also break expectations with an internal job, informative ways, or positive personality change that will help your character stand apart.

Description:

Part historian, entertainer, and conscience, this character has a knack for storytelling through oration, song, poetry, the written word, or other creative means. Their stories often challenge societal norms and raise important questions for listeners.

Fictional Examples:

Scheherazade (One Thousand and One Nights), Joddlor (The Biker), Kaven Blane (City of Ariven), Chopin Trouillefou (Disney's The Hunchback of Notre Dame)

Common Strengths:

CREATIVE CURIOUS EMPATHETIC ENTHUSIASTIC FOCUSED IMAGINATIVE INSPIRATIONAL INTELLIGENT PASSIONATE PENSIVE PERCEPTIVE PERSUASIVE PHILOSOPHICAL PLAYFUL SPONTANEOUS WHIMSICAL WISE WITTY

Common Weaknesses:

CATTY CONFRONTATIONAL GOSSIPY MANIPULATIVE MELODRAMATIC MISCHIEVOUS TEMPERAMENTAL VAIN VERBOSE

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Being gifted at developing complicated plots down to a message people can understand

• Being a natural performer

• Using the sound of their own voice

• Using their gift to shine a light on injustices and right wrongs

• Being theatrical and dramatic

• Dominating conversations

• Hosting their storytelling skills

• Enjoying conversation about culture, art, and important

• Being an avid reader

• Disappearing for story with the people whose story is being told

• Engaging up with current events

• Not being afraid to rattle traditions with their narratives

• Being good at changing a crowd or holding their attention

• Having no ownership or creativity venues (bookshops, coffee, poetry readings, etc.)

• Having an extensive collection of art (books, paintings, DVDs, etc.)

• Being comfortable and living with a lot of creative clutter

• Being able to see an idea from many different angles

• Exaggerating for the sake of impact

• Being a deep thinker

• Enjoying solitude

• Thinking outside the box

• Having your time management

• Struggling with unexpected or change of thought

• Difficulty keeping secrets

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

A SECRET BEING REVEALED BEING WATCHED CONFLICT CROWDS LOSING THE RESPECT OF OTHERS PERSECUTION PUBLIC SPEAKING PUTTING ONESELF OUT THERE

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

• Being told something they're not allowed to repeat

• Their work being stolen

• Having to use their stories as freedom of speech

• Being threatened to remain for sharing certain information

• Suffering to a foreign place where they're unable to communicate with others

• Being turned at to write and tell stories

• Feeling unspired or unprepared

• Seeing another storyteller using their own and manipulate others

• Having to remain anonymous while telling someone's story

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

• Wanting to speak truth but being afraid to challenge the status quo

• Struggling with impostor syndrome

• Being told they have an ongoing tired of always delivering difficult news

• Experiencing a fear of grief that strips their creative abilities

• An injury or illness that makes it impossible for them to tell stories in their preferred way (a writer developing arthritis, a verbal storytelling being their voice, etc.)

• Losing the limelight and having secrets that must be kept in the dark

• Secretly misplacing with the failure as the story they need to tell

Cliches to Be Aware Of:

• The traveling minstrel storyteller

• The storyteller who uses one primary delivery and little variation

• The storyteller as a minor character with no arc, whose only job is to reveal truth to the hero or audience

• Storytellers who rely on traditional methods and are clueless about modern trends

• Storytellers who reveal difficult truths about powerful people without enduring any consequences

Twist This Trope With A Character Who...

• ... has a speech impediment or disability that hinders to themselves

• ... wants to remain anonymous

• ... isn't always a reliable narrator

• ... doesn't have years of extensive looking

• ... uses technology instead of traditional mediums for storytelling

• ... doesn't have typical trait: Vivacious, Vindictive, Devious, etc.
