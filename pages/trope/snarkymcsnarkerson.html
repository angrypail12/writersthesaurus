SNARKY MCSNARKERSON

Helpful Tip: Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping them beyond their predictable layers: beyond, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wound, and related trait and flaw. You can also break expectations with an internal job, informative ways, or positive personality change that will help your character stand apart.

Description:

This highly sarcastic and often cynical character is known for their quick comebacks and deadpan delivery. Skilled at wordplay with a knack for finding the irony in any situation, they employ satire, sarcasm, and humor—often to convey social commentary and criticism.

Fictional Examples:

Artemis Fowl (the Artemis Fowl series), Sherlock Holmes (the Sherlock Holmes Franchise), Tony Stark (Iron Man), Chandler Bing (Friends)

Common Strengths:

ALERT BOLD CALM CONFIDENT CREATIVE FUNNY HONEST INDEPENDENT INTELLIGENT OBJECTIVE OBSERVANT PERCEPTIVE PHILOSOPHICAL PLAYFUL QUIRKY SPONTANEOUS SPUNKY UNINHIBITED WITTY

Common Weaknesses:

ABRASIVE CONFRONTATIONAL CYNICAL DISRESPECTFUL PRETENTIOUS PUSHY RECKLESS SELF-INDULGENT SELFISH TACTLESS

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

• Employing wit and laugh

• Borrowing bright and laugh

• Being sharp-witted

• Naturally resorting to sarcasm

• Excelling at wordplay

• Being funny

• Making cutting observations

• Freely speaking their mind

• Using authentic and pointed or predisposing to what others think

• Making self-deprecating remarks

• Using humor as a weapon—say, when someone opposes the character

• Commenting on irony

• Excelling at wordplay

• Having good comedic timing

• Using sarcasm

• Being able to sniff out hypocrisy and insincerity

• Using humor as a defense mechanism

• Caring more about saying what pleases them for other people's feelings

• Feeling superior to others

• Taking pride in being able to put people in their place

• Always needing to have the last word

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

BEING JUDGED COMPETITION CONFLICT CRITICISM HUMILIATION LOSING THE RESPECT OF OTHERS REGRET REJECTION

Situations That Will Challenge Them:

• Causing trouble for others when the character's target is a priority (a best down, etc.)

• Having to become less cynical person who just wants they out

• The character being asked for advice, and they have no idea what to say

• Being emotionally impaired and unable to think quickly (due to a serious illness, medication, a head trauma, etc.)

• Having to share a difficult truth with a loved one

• Being romantically interested in someone who doesn't get the character's sense of humor

• Being viewed by others as mean or cruel for their jokes

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

• Using their humor to hide a secret, and always fearing it will come to light

• Wanting to graciously connect with others but being too afraid to make themselves vulnerable

• Feeling compelled to reveal a truth but respecting the pain it will bring to the hearer

Cliches to Be Aware Of:

• The smart mouth character where it's their one note and they never reveal any depth

• Flippant wisehiems whose comic dos-top snark becomes a defining characteristic trait

• The caustic and witty vilain who brings all one-liners but never reveals any true vulnerability, complexity of motivation, or depth

• The snarky character who is consistently making people the butt of his jokes, yet they still love him

• Snarky characters who are always bloody and wise

Twist This Trope With A Character Who...

• ... is snarky but also emotionally available and vulnerable

• ... is an unknown despite their dry and snarky humor

• ... is an optimist despite their dry and snarky humor

• ... has a medical reason for their snarky rants—due to a brain condition or their medication relating their inhibitions, for instance

• ... has an atypical trait: analytical, innocent, needy, empathetic, gullible, etc.
