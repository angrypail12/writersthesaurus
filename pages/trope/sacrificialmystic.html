SACRIFICIAL MYSTIC ✪

Description:

This character has a rare and incredible psychic, supernatural, or spiritual ability. But to obtain it, they've had to give up something valuable of their own.

Fictional Examples:

Adam Parrish (The Raven Cycle), Nyx Orcane (Greek mythology), Dinah Bellman (The Langoliers), Eli (The Book of Eli)

Common Strengths:

Courageous Empathetic Focused Generous Honorable Idealistic Independent Inspirational Intelligent Kind Loyal Observant Passionate Perceptive Protective Resourceful Responsible Sensible Spiritual Supportive Talented Unselfish Wise

Common Weaknesses:

Antisocial Cocky Controlling Devious Fanatical Inflexible Irrational Irresponsible Know-It-All Manipulative Martyr Obsessive Pushy Reckless Self-Destructive Superstitious Suspicious Unstable

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

  • Receiving abilities through their actions and world favors or others
  • Wanting to help people
  • Speaking words of wisdom
  • Remaining calm in the face of chaos
  • Warning their parents with humility (not being driven by ego)
  • Viewing themselves as a tool rather than its user
  • Being a good listener
  • Standing up for what they believe in
  • Having a purpose in life that's greater than themselves
  • Moving at a slower pace, not wanting to be rushed
  • Easily reaching people and situations
  • Following their intuition
  • Being open-minded
  • Finding excitement, not being dull or the past
  • Being mystical or spiritual
  • Refusing small details about a person or situation
  • Struggling with the price and cost before making a decision
  • Wanting to help to sacrifice for a greater cause
  • Being a loner
  • Living simply
  • Being a loner

Fears That Might Limit the Character:

Becoming a Parent Becoming What One Hates Being a Burden to Others Being Capable of Harm Being Forced Being Taken Advantage Of Criticism Government Humiliation Losing Authority Losing One's Mind

Helpful Tip:

Using a trope or archetype helps readers quickly understand a character, but stepping down creates a weak and predictable figure. Instead, add depth with a unique backstory, emotional wounds, and context that creates an arc to potential growth.
