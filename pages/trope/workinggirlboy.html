WORKING GIRL OR BOY ⚹
Back to Character Types and Tropes

Description:

This character is young, ambitious, and ready to take on the world. Whether they're working their first job post school or are striking out in a new career field, they're driven to succeed.

Fictional Examples:

Andy Sachs (The Devil Wears Prada), Nick Carraway (The Great Gatsby), Peggy Olson (Mad Men), Tess McGill (Working Girl)

Common Strengths:

Ambitious Appreciative Confident Cooperative Courteous Creative Disciplined Efficient Enthusiastic Focused Friendly Honest Humble Idealistic Industrious Kind Observant Optimistic Persistent Proactive Professional Proper Resourceful Responsible Sensible Spunky Wholesome

Common Weaknesses:

Childish Needy Nervous Oversensitive Perfectionist Workaholic

Associated Actions, Behaviors, and Tendencies:

Being goal-oriented
Viewing their circumstances with optimism
Having big dreams
Being idealistic
Working diligently with energy and determination
Having something to prove
Putting in long hours, working hard
Lacking years to share
Boldly seizing new opportunities that arise
Seeking to establish themselves in their field, especially those who can help them succeed
Learning new skills to develop and improve their chances of success
Being entitled to clash with superiors or colleagues who are stuck in their ways
Adapting to new challenges
Being resilient in the face of adversity
Seeking mentorship (love relationships, personal conflicts, etc.) to achieve their goals
Being optimistic in their potential while also making their career ambitions and their personal life
Being willing to take risks
Being problem-solvers
Feeling motivated
Being unable or unwilling to see some people's faults
Being overhositic about other people to get what they want
Changing themselves to suit others instead of being true to who they are

Fears that Might Limit the Character:

Being Watched Certain Kinds Of People Criticism Failure Leading Letting Others Down Not Being In Control Not Fitting In One's Instincts Being Unreliable Putting Oneself Out There Rejection

Situations that Will Challenge Them:

Being betrayed by someone they'd trusted as a mentor or guide
Realizing that success will require sacrifices they're not willing to make
Having their ideas shot down or co-opted by a jealous co-worker
Being passed over for professional opportunities they feel they deserved
Learning they're going to be fired about their professional dreams
Chasing a promotion that a friend is also pursuing
Realizing that success will require a skill that is actually the character's weakness

Inner Struggles to Give Them Depth:

Wanting the goal but suffering from burnout and exhaustion
Struggling with imposter syndrome
Craving external validation for their abilities, which doesn't result in fulfillment
Craving work-based validation that often career ambitions and their personal life
Competing themselves to others and always coming up short
Yearning their own personal values in terms of their professional accomplishments, and struggling with feelings of low self-worth

Clichés to be Aware Of:

The persistent, ambitious dreamer with a lot of potential who gains great success through hard work and persistence
The workaholic whose job overrides their career who will sacrifice so they can have a happy and devoted family
The workaholic girl or boy who chastity drives men wild or "saved" by a love interest who shows them that sacrifices

Twist this Trope with a Character Who...

Is pursuing an unconventional career, such as circus ring master, pyracy, or professional gaming
Is secretly wealthy and is driven by a mysterious other than getting rich
Has a physical difference that makes success more challenging, like a health condition or a world-specific challenge (having blue skin or empowerment)
Is willing to do anything to be famous via unethical methods, such as blackmailing important or forcing them to remove them as obstacles
Has worrying about they've learned via unethical methods via their career ambitions and their personal life
Has an atypical trait: Philosophical, Self-Indulgent, Violinist, etc.
