SLIMY

HELPFUL TIP

Textures are an excellent way to bring readers deeper into the scene. As you freshly describe textures found in the setting, readers will be reminded of their own past experiences. Used in conjunction with colors and textures or shapes, the object associated with the texture can become easier to imagine, especially when dealing with something abstract.

Practical Examples:
Describing texture in a story is a powerful way to pull readers into a scene. Not only does it give depth to specific setting details, it can actually create intimacy between a reader and character. Because textures are universal, describing how something feels to the POV character can evoke a sense of shared experience in a reader. To anchor your audience in the scene using textures, make sure that comparisons and contrasts are clear and relatable. Choose textures that are naturally a part of the character or narrator's life knowledge and experiences.
A WEAK EXAMPLE:
The pond's fish market was something to see—plastic tube filled with octopus and squid, their dripping, ropey tentacles hanging from the sides, months of greasy slimed scum on the tables, mounds of greasy slimed debris. Salted fish Blacksmiths.
WHAT'S WRONG WITH THIS EXAMPLE?
Lots of good, slimy imagery, but that's the problem... textures experienced by the POV character are most effectively conveyed when shown through touch.
A STRONGER OPTION:
At the fish market, the sea was like a trick to the flow, thick with the stench of blood and rotten innards. Men in rubber aprons whistled cheeses with precision, hopping up pieces into wet, clumsy heaps. At the end of the row I passed my goal—jumbo head filled with fresh oysters, their being a treasure that this smeary hop would be worth it. So much time wandering, so much money squandered, and Marcus wouldn't be able to resist my Oysters Rockefeller.
WHY DOES THIS EXAMPLE WORK?
In a highly sensory environment, using multiple senses can make a more vivid image than using one sense alone. Here, the multisensory descriptions work together to evoke the emotional response of disgust, which reinforces the character's determination to create the perfect meal to woo Marcus.
