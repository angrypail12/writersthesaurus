CHARACTER MOTIVATION THESAURUS

ACHIEVING DOMINION OVER ANOTHER (DARK)
  • My Workspace
  • Thunder Runners




HELPFUL TIP:

To understand why your character is driven toward a certain goal, get to know their positive and negative traits, as well as any significant emotional wounds that might be motivating them. Then you can explore the various kinds of conflict that could block them as they move toward their goal.
NOTES:
Power and control is a central goal of many antagonists and some anti-heroes. In this case, the goal is to achieve complete control over one individual. Keep in mind that the methods for control will be vastly different depending on what type of control the character seeks.

FORMS THIS MIGHT TAKE:
Enslaving someone
Making another dependent on oneself for survival
Trapping a rival so they are under one's complete control
Holding someone captive
Kidnapping
Forcible confinement
Having control over another person's mortality (life and death decision-making)
Having complete control over a spouse, lover, or child
Controlling another person's fate in a significant way
Steering a child's focus in life (to become a hockey star, to develop into a musical prodigy, etc.)

HUMAN NEED DRIVING THE GOAL (INNER MOTIVATION):
There are five basic human needs that, when missing from a character's life, could motivate them to pursue this goal. The following needs are all possibilities, but only one of them should be the primary driver for any given character. For more information on the relationship between human needs and outer motivations, please see this Character Motivation tutorial.

Self-Actualization: A character who is narcissistic in nature may believe that only through control can they truly be revered as they believe they should be. Alternatively, a parent who was robbed of a career or opportunity may feel they can still attain a higher purpose if their talented child achieves this dream in their place.
Esteem and Recognition: A character looking to show others how powerful he is will seek dominion over another to prove their own worth.
Love and Belonging: A character seeking the approval and acceptance of a powerful person or group may pursue total control over another to prove they too belong in the same circle.
Safety and Security: A character who truly believes only they can keep another safe may see dominion as being "for the other person's own good."
Physiological Needs: In a kill-or-be-killed situation, a character who feels threatened may strike first and remove another person's power so they cease to be a threat.

HOW THE CHARACTER MAY PREPARE FOR THIS GOAL:
Researching the target and determining their weaknesses
Obtaining items needed to gain access (pass codes, keys, credentials, etc.)
Removing obstacles that could restrict one's access to the target
Shutting out distracting forces around the target
Working to secure a position of trust by providing value and much-needed help
Purchasing materials to kidnap and hold the target
Showing kindness and compassion to disarm the target and encourage trust
Building a secure facility to hold one's target
Enlisting the help of others (guards, watchers, etc.)
Manipulation
Quietly working against them so they will fail, causing low self-confidence and self-esteem
Brainwashing techniques
Torture
Causing an injury (e.g., breaking a bone to discourage escape)
Using love and affection as a weapon
Sowing discord as a way of cutting off the target's other relationships
Isolation techniques
Encouraging dependency (emotional, financial, etc.)
Exposing the target to danger so the character can "rescue" them or wear down their strengths
Holding back information or feeding the target and their allies misinformation
Removing their safety net (friends and family members who support them, financial assets and savings, much-needed resources, etc.)
Using rumors and misinformation or manufacturing situations that will damage the person's reputation and cause allies to distance themselves
Encouraging destructive habits (drugs, drinking, gambling, taking risks, etc.) to weaken the target or create addictions and dependency
Wearing the person down through a lack of sleep, over-stimulation, illness, or other means
Gas-lighting to cause the target to doubt themselves and their memory and skew their worldview (to isolate them or create dependency)
Using guilt, shame, and humiliation to weaken the target
Turning the target's children against them to add to their isolation
Assuming a provider role to encourage further dependence
Restricting the target's movements, access to information, and mental stimulation
Rewarding behavior (such as obeying instructions) that reinforces the character's control and power
Removing all avenues of escape and independence (e.g., keeping the person prisoner and not giving them access to money, freedom, or things that could lead to freedom)
Holding the target's loved ones hostage as insurance
Holding damaging evidence in trust to ensure compliance
Using threats and coercion
Using violence and abuse
Restricting food, medicine, or other necessities to weaken the target
Limiting the target's opportunities to a singular focus
Rewarding obedience and punishing disobedience
Threatening to take away what they want most to force them to comply
Using guilt to ensure the person does what the character wishes (common with characters who are living their life through their children, for example)

POSSIBLE SACRIFICES OR COSTS ASSOCIATED WITH THIS GOAL:
An increased financial burden (to pay bribes, keep the target a prisoner, ensure that the target is isolated, etc.)
Losing time, resources, and energy if the character's target is especially hard to capture
Becoming obsessed with the goal to the point that other motivations must be given up
Having to live somewhat isolated to keep authorities or the target's supporters away
Becoming obsessed with the goal, and in doing so, revealing a blind spot that a rival could use against the character

ROADBLOCKS WHICH COULD PREVENT THIS GOAL FROM BEING ACHIEVED:
Authorities being alerted by concerned neighbors
A spouse or child rebelling in public, drawing attention to the character's controlling behaviors
The target trying to escape
The target successfully drawing attention to their situation (through a note, a whispered plea, an attempted escape) or involving outsiders (e.g., starting a fire to alert the fire department)
The target growing ill and needing medical attention, which could expose mistreatment or provide an opportunity for escape
Supporters of the target rallying and attempting a rescue
A nosy neighbor
Being caught trying to meddle in the target's life (stealing or destroying paperwork, being caught in a lie, a bribe being refused, etc.)
Distant relatives that come calling out of the blue
A delivery driver or maintenance repair person who sees something off and becomes suspicious
A target with a strong will that refuses to be broken
A lack of leverage over the target
Falling out of political favor and not having the back-up needed to gain the desired level of control
A friend or loved one of the target who refuses to be deterred in their quest to find out what's really going on
Choosing a target that is well-connected and who will be missed
Unrelated criminal activity in the area that brings a canvassing police officer to the character's door
A witness coming forward
Leaving a paper trail or being caught on a surveillance camera

TALENTS & SKILLS THAT WILL HELP THE CHARACTER ACHIEVE THIS GOAL:
A KNACK FOR MAKING MONEY, CHARM, ENHANCED HEARING, ESP (CLAIRVOYANCE), EXCEPTIONAL MEMORY, GAINING THE TRUST OF OTHERS, HOT-WIRING A CAR, KNIFE THROWING, KNOWLEDGE OF EXPLOSIVES, LIP-READING, LYING, MECHANICALLY INCLINED, MENTALISM, MULTITASKING, PHOTOGRAPHIC MEMORY, READING PEOPLE, SELF-DEFENSE, SHARPSHOOTING, SLEIGHT OF HAND, STRATEGIC THINKING, SUPER STRENGTH, SURVIVAL SKILLS, SWIFT-FOOTEDNESS, WILDERNESS NAVIGATION, WRESTLING

WHAT'S AT STAKE IF THIS GOAL ISN'T MET?
Losing the character's standing among others
Having the character's position of power or access taken away
Being incarcerated
Losing what the character wants most
Lifelong regret and unhappiness
Self-loathing
Dying
Being cast aside by the target

CLICHS TO AVOID:
The antagonist seeking power over another because they "lust for power." There should always be an underlying reason why a character so desperately wants to control another, so make sure this comes through as you deliver backstory through behavior, the character's skewed worldview, and snippets of information.
