FOREST FIRE ☆

HELPFUL TIP:

Don't be afraid to pair weather conditions with your setting choice to contrast or reinforce a character's emotions. Replacements might be mirrored in the relentless drizzle of rain and the resulting heavy drag of one's clothes; alternatively, the feeling can stand out in stark contrast to sunlight dancing over fresh snow. Can weather also provide symbolism, thereby enriching the reader's experience? Think about your goal for the scene and the mood you are trying to create, and then layer your description with meaningful choices.

REINFORCING A MOOD:
A forest fire evokes high anxiety and places characters in a fight for their lives. Fire leaves no prisoners; it burns, eradicates, kills. The intensity of such destruction and the fear of burning both reinforce a state of hell. In the backdrop of such an event, all desires, conflicts, and goals are forgotten in the only thing that matters: survival.
SYMBOLISM:
DEATH, DISORDER, GREED, CONTROL, CORRUPTION, REVENGE, SUFFERING, TRANSFORMATION, CLEANSING
COMMON CLICHÉS:
  • A fire starting from a discarded cigarette
  • Characters hiding from enemies so they stay a fire
WEATHER NOTES:
Forest fires can happen anywhere and anytime, but most occur during the dry summer months. Though they can be a product of man or nature, statistically, most are caused by lightning strikes.
