Clouds

Sights

  • Sunset in with streaks of pink or orange
  • Storm-dark blisters blended with rain
  • Silver margins
  • Patches of sunlight that create shades through the gray
  • Wispy or scant curls that slide across the moon and stars
  • Glimpses that shore call wild light as shadows bloom
  • Low banks that hug the foothills
  • Gray clouds in a gray sky

Textures and Sensations

Clouds are generally only felt as a cloud shadow because if one were able to touch the clouds, they would likely feel cold and damp.

Sounds

Thunder may accompany certain cloud formations.

Reinforcing a Mood

The type of cloud can dictate what sort of mood is present in a scene. Puffy white clouds are often associated with happy memories of love days, vacations, being outdoors, and good sunshine; they can evoke feelings of contentment, joy, and optimism. Quick-moving clouds, usually accompanied by wind, can bring about a feeling that change is coming. Dark clouds that pack the sky or seem to create a wall of shadow may set an ominous mood, heightening anxiety or fear. On the other hand, rain-filled clouds can also bring relief or excitement in an area that has been oppressed by heat as drought, so consider carefully the context of the scene.

Symbolism

CHANGE, DOMINION, DEPRESSION, INNOCENCE, HIDING OR REVEALING INFORMATION, OPPRESSION

Common Clichés

  • A bank of clouds parting to let the sun shine down on a happy occasion or event
  • A storm that troubles a villain's appearance or catastrophic event
  • Describing clouds as "cotton balls"

Weather Notes

Different weather conditions will dictate the type of clouds that form. Choose imagery that fits the scene, but if a specific weather event (such as tornado) occurs, research the clouds that typically appear before, during, and after to ensure accuracy.

Scenarios for Adding Conflict or Tension

  • Using clouds to lower the temperature, making an outdoor event (a day at the beach, a graduation party, a wedding, etc.) less comfortable
  • Using low cloud cover to obscure the landscape, hiding an important landmark that a character is seeking
  • Packing a sky with clouds at the most possible time in order to hide a dangerous threat (such as enemy fighter jets or stealth aircraft that are invisible to radar)
