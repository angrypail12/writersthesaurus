Reinforcing a Mood

Dusk signals the start of night and an end to the stresses that have built up throughout the day—a time when characters can wind down and move toward a period of rest and relaxation. The weather at dusk, as at any time of day, can help influence the mood. A rainy, windy dusk may close conversations and dependence as a stormy day turns to stormy darkness. Breezy, airy dusks may lull the spirits. For maximum impact, pair a breeze with other weather elements to enhance the mood for readers.
