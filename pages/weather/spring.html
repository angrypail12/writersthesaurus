SPRING ☆

HELPFUL TIP:

Don't be afraid to pair weather conditions with your setting choice to contrast or reinforce a character's emotions. Replacements might be mirrored in the relentless drizzle of rain and the resulting heavy drag of one's clothes; alternatively, the feeling can stand out in stark contrast to sunlight dancing over fresh snow. Can weather also provide symbolism, thereby enriching the reader's experience? Think about your goal for the scene and the mood you are trying to create, and then layer your description with meaningful choices.

  • Dormant plants pushing up around the yard
  • Buds and blossoms forming on trees
  • Robins nesting

REINFORCING A MOOD:
After so many months of cold weather and brown or snow-covered landscapes, spring brings a renewed sense of optimism. Spirits lift, people are more friendly and kind. Spring evokes hope and vigor.
SYMBOLISM:
REBIRTH, BEGINNINGS, BEAUTY, HEALTH, HOPE
COMMON CLICHÉS:
Calling someone a "spring chicken"
WEATHER NOTES:
Weather and seasons vary by region. Spring in Canada looks very different from spring in southern California. Temperate areas may have a very short spring, if any at all.

  • Spring and the mood of second chances might grate against a character who loses something, turning a beginning into an ending
