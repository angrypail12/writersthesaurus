Reinforcing a Mood

  • A blizzard can reinforce a feeling of isolation, either for a character or an entire community.
  • The raw power of nature also conveys the importance of safety and security.
  • Blizzards have the ability to bring people together. The feeling of being confined can help characters find common ground and strengthen fellowship or do the opposite—creating friction and clauses that increase tension.

Weather Notes

Blizzards are classified as long-duration snowstorms with sustained winds of at least 35 mph that produces reduced visibility of about 500 meters or less. What is often called a "blizzard" expected does not qualify as a storm after only a small amount of time, creating a 'frontline' and possibly death. Visibility is so reduced that a character can be a few feet from safety and not realize it—becoming lost. This type of storm also affects snow within a short period of time, and may completely psyche a populated area by knocking out power and making travel difficult or impossible. When writing a scene of this magnitude into a story, do your research to make sure its severity fits your fictional setting.

Scenarios for Adding Conflict or Tension

  • A blizzard forcing enemies to rely on one another for survival
  • Having a family secret come out as people are trapped together
  • Being prepared for the storm but having to flee one's refuge when something unexpected happens (a house fire, a home invasion, etc.)
