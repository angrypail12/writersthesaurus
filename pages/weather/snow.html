SNOW ☆

HELPFUL TIP:

Don't be afraid to pair weather conditions with your setting choice to contrast or reinforce a character's emotions. Replacements might be mirrored in the relentless drizzle of rain and the resulting heavy drag of one's clothes; alternatively, the feeling can stand out in stark contrast to sunlight dancing over fresh snow. Can weather also provide symbolism, thereby enriching the reader's experience? Think about your goal for the scene and the mood you are trying to create, and then layer your description with meaningful choices.

  • Snow threading through the trees to frost branches and bushes
  • Thick layers of white accumulating on roofs, cars, and walkways
  • Cold winds forcing the snow into drifts
  • Footprints making a fresh path to people walk to the bus or take their dogs out
  • Small, undetectable flakes (rain mixed, and barely visible) that are barely visible on the snow crust
  • Animal prints snaking their paths up the cold into mounded and rounded snow
  • Tufts of dead grass poking up through the white
  • Sunlight sparkling off snow with glittering display
  • Crusting wind that causes branches to shift and snow to cascade down in a drift
  • People shoveling sidewalks and driveways
  • Road salt melting the ice at bus stops
  • Shovels leaning against a porch railing
  • Children building snowmen and forts
  • Bright sleds being ridden down a steep hill

TASTES:

REINFORCING A MOOD:
Snow tends to lighten one's mood and bring back happy memories of childhood: sledding, making snowmen, playing outdoors, etc. As such, it's often viewed as a gift from nature and has the power to pull people out of their dark thoughts long enough to notice and appreciate its beauty. WEATHER NOTES:
Snow only occurs in certain climates and begins to melt when the temperature rises above freezing. Some climates, such as tropical locations, never see snow at all. Where snow does fall, when the temperature hovers just below freezing, the accumulation will often be heavier and wetter than that occurs in the bitter cold.
SCENARIOS FOR ADDING CONFLICT OR TENSION:
  • If too much snow accumulates it can damage structures, collapsing roofs and damaging property, wreaking havoc for characters
  • If a character is ill-prepared on a road trip, a heavy snowfall could trap him in a car, creating a life-threatening in a dangerous situation
  • The blanket of beauty can be quite deceptive, hiding steep ravines, lakes, and other dangers. If your character takes one wrong step, he could find himself below the surface, fighting for his life
