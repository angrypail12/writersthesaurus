RAIN ☆

HELPFUL TIP:

Don't be afraid to pair weather conditions with your setting choice to contrast or reinforce a character's emotions. Replacements might be mirrored in the relentless drizzle of rain and the resulting heavy drag of one's clothes; alternatively, the feeling can stand out in stark contrast to sunlight dancing over fresh snow. Can weather also provide symbolism, thereby enriching the reader's experience? Think about your goal for the scene and the mood you are trying to create, and then layer your description with meaningful choices.

REINFORCING A MOOD:
Unless it follows hot weather, rain triggers a fight response—fear from fear that the desire to not be inconvenienced. To avoid the cold, pelting rain, people will hurry to find shelter until the rain passes. Long-term rain can create a feeling of gloom that might bring a person down. If the weather has been very hot, then rain can create a sense of relief and release. This can have strong applications within a novel as it mirrors moments of tension, both internal and external.
SYMBOLISM:
DEPRESSION, ENJOYMENT, REBIRTH, HEALTH, PURITY, REDEMPTION, CLEANSING
COMMON CLICHÉS:
  • Including a backdrop of rain when tears are flowing
  • Using rain as a conduit to show a depressed character's emotional landscape
  • Using rain to imply an evil or soulless evil
WEATHER NOTES:
Rain occurs when humidity reaches a breaking point and happens most frequently during the spring months. Rain can last for minutes, hours, or days depending on the size of the storm cell. Before rain hits, the air may sweep pressurized or heavy. Summer rains tend to be hard but of a shorter duration while spring rains are often needier and longer lasting, which can lead to rivers rising and flooding.
SCENARIOS FOR ADDING CONFLICT OR TENSION:
  • Being caught in the rain can increase someone's discomfort or inconvenience (having to wear soaking wet clothes, one's makeup running and hair going flat when a character wants to look her best, running one's expensive bag or shoes, having to walk or bicycle a long distance in the rain)
  • Rain naturally slows things down and causes delays: Road washouts, blocked roadways caused by accidents, and forced detours can frustrate travellers, especially those who are in a hurry
  • Rain during a harvest could delay crops being picked or transport, which drives up prices and causes shortages. If the crop is important for a specific celebration or event, this could dampen the spirits of a large group of people. (Imagine a town's combination without mead, for example)
