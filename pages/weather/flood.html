REINFORCING A MOOD:
Floods are destructive and can produce feelings of helplessness and despair. They also reveal the duplicity of nature—that the very thing people live on can become a powerful force that threatens. In the threatening situations, a flood often means people in consider the big picture and motivate them to re-order their priorities. Floods can also reinforce the importance of people as a resource, when their generosity and willingness to work together overcome some of the havoc systems of the human race.

WEATHER NOTES:
Floods have a variety of causes: heavy rainfall, snowmelt in rivers, over-saturated soil, dam or levee failure, etc. Some floods occur seasonally in certain areas while others are completely unexpected. Flash floods are the most devastating in that they occur suddenly without warning, giving people very little time to seek higher ground. Usually, evacuations in streams and rivers occur with heavy rainfall.
